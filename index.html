The Problem We're Solving

Grief should not be exploited.

When a loved one passes, families in Singapore are often thrust into negotiations with funeral directors within hours — exhausted, emotional, and unfamiliar with true costs. Casket pricing is deliberately opaque, buried inside all-inclusive packages impossible to break down.

"We were quoted S$3,800 as part of the 'standard package'. We only found out afterwards that the casket alone was marked up from a S$600 cost price. We had no time, no energy to question it. We just paid."

— Testimonial from a bereaved family in Tampines, 2023
🪄

Opaque Package Pricing

Caskets are bundled into funeral packages with no itemised breakdown, making it impossible to compare prices or exercise consumer choice.

⏳

Emotional Time Pressure

Arrangements must often be made within hours of a death. Directors know families are in no state to research alternatives. Pressure tactics are common.

🚪

Service Withdrawal Threats

Some directors threaten to withhold services if families decline their in-house casket. Families deserve to know they can and should challenge this.
